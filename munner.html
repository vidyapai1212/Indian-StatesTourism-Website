Munnar

Munnar is a beautiful hill station in Kerala.It is famous for tea gardens, cool climate, and scenic mountains.Many tourists visit Munnar every year.Munnar, the "Kashmir of South India," is renowned for its vast, rolling tea plantations, cool climate, and dramatic misty mountains. Located at the confluence of three rivers (Muthirapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala), it is a major South Indian hill station famous for the 12-year blooming cycle of the Neelakurinji flower and trekking in Eravikulam National Park. Key Specialities of Munnar: Tea Plantations & Gardens: Sprawling, lush green tea estates cover the landscape, offering scenic views and fresh tea factory tours. Eravikulam National Park: Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and offering breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. Neelakurinji Bloom: Famous for the rare blue flower that blooms once every 12 years, turning the hillsides blue. Scenic Viewpoints & Waterfalls: Highlights include Top Station (panoramic views), Echo Point, and numerous, dramatic waterfalls such as Chinnakanal. Colonial Heritage: Many of the bungalows and structures in the town are old British colonial country cottages. Adventure Tourism: Popular for trekking, hiking, plantation walks, and wildlife spotting in surrounding sanctuaries. Climate: Known as a romantic, refreshing getaway with temperatures ranging from 15–25°C. Munnar, located in Kerala's Idukki district at the confluence of three rivers, was developed in the late 19th century as a British tea plantation hub. Originally inhabited by the Muthuvan tribe, the area was developed by pioneers like A.H. Sharp, who introduced tea in the 1880s, transforming the region into a major plantation center
