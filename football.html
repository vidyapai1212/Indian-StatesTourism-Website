Development of Modern Football The 19th century saw formal rules emerge in English public schools, culminating in the 1863 creation of the Football Association (FA), which established soccer as distinct from rugby. Key early milestones included Sheffield FC's founding (1857) and the first FA Cup in 1872. International competition, notably between Scotland and England, grew, and the sport rapidly professionalized in the 1880s Ancient Origins and Early Forms The earliest known form of a kicking ball game is Cuju, played in China during the Han Dynasty (206 BCE – 220 CE). Cuju involved kicking a leather ball stuffed with feathers into a small net and was used for military training. Ancient Greeks played Episkyros, a team game that involved both throwing and kicking a ball. Romans practiced Harpastum, a high-intensity game that emphasized strategy and teamwork. Indigenous peoples in Mesoamerica played ball games with rubber balls as early as 3,000 years ago. The Japanese developed Kemari around 600 AD, a non-competitive game where players kept a ball in the air. In medieval Europe, "mob football" was common, often involving entire villages playing with very few rules. These matches were frequently violent and could span miles between competing towns. Authorities in England repeatedly attempted to ban football due to its disruptive and chaotic nature. King Edward II issued the first known ban in 1314 to prevent "great tumults" in public fields. Despite these bans, the game remained a popular pastime for the common people of Britain. The Birth of Modern Football The 19th century brought industrialization and a move toward more organized leisure activities. English public schools, such as Eton, Harrow, and Rugby, developed their own distinct versions of the game. In 1845, pupils at Rugby School produced the first set of written rules for their version of the sport. The Cambridge Rules of 1848 were an early attempt to unify different school codes. On 24 October 1857, Sheffield F.C. was founded, now recognized as the world's oldest independent club. The Football Association (FA) was established in London on 26 October 1863. This founding meeting marked the official birth of modern association football. The FA's first "Laws of the Game" banned carrying the ball with hands and "hacking" (kicking shins). This decision led to a permanent split between association football and rugby. The FA Cup, established in 1871, became the first major competitive football tournament. The first official international match was played between Scotland and England in 1872. Professionalism was formally legalized by the FA in 1885 after years of secret payments to players. In 1888, the Football League was founded in England as the first national league competition
