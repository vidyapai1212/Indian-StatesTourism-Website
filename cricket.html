Cricket

Cricket is the most popular sport in India. India has won many international tournaments. Cricket originated in southeast England, likely during medieval times, evolving from a children's game into an adult sport by the early 17th century. It became popular in the 18th century, with the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) forming in 1787. The sport spread globally via the British Empire, establishing international test matches by 1877. Origins and Early Development Early Roots: Evidence suggests cricket was played as early as 1550 in Surrey, England. It likely originated in the "Weald" region of Kent and Sussex. Name Origin: Possible derivations include the Middle Dutch krick(-e) (stick), Old English cricc or cryce (crutch/staff), or French criquet (wooden post). 17th–18th Century: Originally a rural pastime, it grew in popularity, with the first known county teams emerging in the early 18th century. The first Laws of Cricket were written in 1744. Hambledon and MCC: The Hambledon Club was the game's focal point for 30 years until the MCC was founded in 1787, establishing its headquarters at Lord's Cricket Ground. Legendary Figures Sir Donald Bradman: Widely considered the greatest Test batsman with a career average of 99.94. Sachin Tendulkar: The first player to score 100 international centuries and the highest run-scorer in Test and ODI cricket. Sir Garfield Sobers: Known as one of the best all-rounders in cricket history. Brian Lara: Holds the record for the highest individual score in a Test innings (400*). Virat Kohli & MS Dhoni: Modern icons who led India to multiple titles and sustained dominance. The game has grown from its humble English origins into a global phenomenon with a fan base of over one billion people.
