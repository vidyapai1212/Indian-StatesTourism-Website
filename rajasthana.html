Rajasthan Tourist Places

1. Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, is a 5-story pink sandstone "Palace of Winds" in Jaipur. Its 953 jharokhas (small windows) allowed royal women to observe street life, such as processions, without being seen. It is a masterpiece of Rajput architecture, shaped like Lord Krishna's crown, and designed for natural cooling.

Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

2. Amber Fort

Amer Fort in Jaipur is renowned as a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its stunning blend of16th-century Rajput and Mughal architecture, opulent palaces, and, most notably, the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace). Built from yellow/pink sandstone and marble, it is famous for its intricate carvings, hilltop location over Maota Lake, and, traditionally, elephant rides.

Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

View Location

3. Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort, known as the Sonar Quila or Golden Fort, is the premier landmark of Jaisalmer, Rajasthan, built in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a rare "living fort," with thousands of residents, shops, and hotels inside its yellow sandstone walls, offering stunning views of the Thar Desert.

Location: Jaisalmer Fort, Rajasthan

View Location

4. City Palace Udaipur

The City Palace in Jaipur is famous for its stunning fusion of Rajput and Mughal architecture, serving as the18th-century royal seat of the Kachwaha dynasty. It is renowned for the Chandra Mahal (current royal residence), the Pritam Niwas Chowk's four themed gates, the City Palace Museum (displaying royal costumes/weapons), and its iconic pink-hued, intricately designed courtyards and gardens.

Location: City Palace Udaipur, Rajasthan
