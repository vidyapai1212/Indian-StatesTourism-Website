Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance forms of India. It originated in the temples of Tamil Nadu and was traditionally performed as a devotional dance dedicated to Lord Shiva and other deities. History of Bharatanatyam Bharatanatyam developed more than 2000 years ago and was performed by temple dancers called Devadasis. The dance combines expression, music, rhythm, and storytelling. Meaning of Bharatanatyam The word Bharatanatyam is made from: Bha – Bhava (Expression) Ra – Raga (Music) Ta – Tala (Rhythm) Natyam – Dance Dance Elements Bharatanatyam includes: • Mudras (Hand gestures) • Abhinaya (Facial expressions) • Footwork and body movements • Classical Carnatic music accompaniment Costume and Performance Dancers wear colorful silk costumes, traditional jewelry, and ankle bells called Ghungroo. The dance expresses stories from Hindu mythology such as Ramayana and Mahabharata. Modern Popularity Today Bharatanatyam is performed worldwide on cultural stages, festivals, and competitions. It is an important symbol of Indian culture and heritage.
