Kandariya Mahadeva Temple

Kandariya Mahadeva Temple is a famous temple in Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh. It is known for its beautiful carvings and ancient architecture. The temple was built during the Chandela dynasty. The Kandariya Mahadeva Temple in Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh, is the largest and most ornate Hindu temple in the UNESCO World Heritage site, built around 1025–1050 CE by the Chandela King Vidyadhara. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it was constructed to commemorate victory over Mahmud of Ghazni, representing the peak of Nagara-style architecture. Historical Highlights: Patron & Construction: Built by King Vidyadhara, a powerful Chandela ruler (reigned c. 1003–1035 CE), to celebrate his success against invaders, including Mahmud of Ghazni. Dedication: The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, with a marble linga in the sanctum acting as the central feature. Name Origin: "Kandariya Mahadeva" translates to the "Great God of the Cave," signifying Shiva as the deity. Architectural Significance: It is considered the finest example of Central Indian temple architecture, featuring a 31-meter-high main tower (Shikhara) surrounded by 84 smaller, interlinked spires, mimicking the shape of Mount Kailash. Rediscovery: Following the decline of the Chandela dynasty in the 12th century, the temple fell into obscurity until its rediscovery by British official T.S. Burt in 1838.
