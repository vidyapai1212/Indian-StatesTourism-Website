Maharashtra Tourist Places

1. Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is a famous, 85-foot-tall arch-monument in Mumbai, completed in 1924 to commemorate King George V and Queen Mary's 1911 visit. Located on the Apollo Bunder waterfront, this Indo-Saracenic structure is an iconic landmark known for its historic significance, bustling atmosphere, and stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra

2. Ajanta Caves

Ajanta Caves are ancient rock-cut caves famous for Buddhist paintings and sculptures.he Ajanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage site featuring 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments carved into a horseshoe-shaped cliff in Maharashtra, India, dating from the 2nd century BCE to the 6th century CE. Famous for masterpieces of ancient Indian art, they contain vibrant murals, frescoes, and sculptures depicting Jataka tales and the life of Buddha.

Location: Aurangabad District

View Location

3. Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves are historic caves known for beautiful temple carvings and architecture.The Ellora Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Maharashtra, India, represent an extraordinary complex of 34 rock-cut temples and monasteries carved between 600 and 1000 CE. Showcasing religious harmony, they feature 12 Buddhist, 17 Hindu (including the massive monolithic Kailasa Temple), and 5 Jain caves.

Location: Aurangabad District

View Location

4. Mahabaleshwar

Mahabaleshwar is a famous hill station known for its cool climate and scenic views.Mahabaleshwar, the "Queen of Sahyadri Hills," is a premier, scenic hill station in Maharashtra's Satara district, situated at 1,353 meters in the Western Ghats. Known for its lush greenery, strawberry farms, and ancient temples, it offers year-round attractions like Arthur's Seat, Venna Lake, and Pratapgad Fort, making it a popular weekend destination from Mumbai and Pune.

Location: Satara District
