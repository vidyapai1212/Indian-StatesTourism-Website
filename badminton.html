Badminton

Badminton is very popular in India. Many players like P. V. Sindhu and Saina Nehwal are world champions. Badminton is a fast-paced racquet sport that challenges both the body and mind. Known as the world's fastest racquet sport, it requires players to exhibit exceptional speed, agility, and precision to hit a shuttlecock across a high net. Whether played for casual recreation or at a professional Olympic level, badminton offers a comprehensive workout and a rewarding test of strategy. History and Origin The modern game of badminton traces its roots back to the 19th century in British India, where it was known as "Poona". British military officers stationed there introduced a net to the traditional game of "battledore and shuttlecock". Upon returning to England in the 1870s, the sport was popularized at Badminton House, the estate of the Duke of Beaufort, which eventually gave the sport its permanent name. It was officially included in the Olympic Games starting in 1992.
