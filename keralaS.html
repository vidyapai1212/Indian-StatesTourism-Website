Kerala Tourist Places

1. Munnar

Munnar is a beautiful hill station famous for tea plantations and cool climate.Munnar, a premier hill station in Kerala, is famous for its vast, rolling tea plantations, mist-covered mountains, and scenic biodiversity in the Western Ghats. Often called the "Kashmir of South India," it is renowned for the Eravikulam National Park (home to the Nilgiri Tahr), the rare Neelakurinji flower, and attractions like Top Station, Mattupetty Dam, and the Tea Museum.

Location: Munnar, Kerala

2. Alleppey Backwaters

Alleppey is famous for houseboat cruises and peaceful backwaters.Alleppey (now Alappuzha) is world-famous as the "Venice of the East" due to its intricate, palm-fringed network of canals, lagoons, and rivers. It serves as the primary gateway to the Kerala backwaters, offering a unique ecosystem where freshwater rivers meet the Arabian Sea.

Location: Alappuzha, Kerala

3. Wayanad

Wayanad is known for forests, waterfalls and wildlife sanctuaries.Wayanad, a scenic district in Kerala, is famous for its lush Western Ghats landscapes, misty tea plantations, and ancient 6,000-year-old Edakkal Caves. It is a top destination for nature lovers, offering treks to Chembra Peak, wildlife spotting in Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, and adventures at Banasura Sagar Dam.

Location: Wayanad, Kerala

4. Kovalam Beach

Kovalam Beach is a famous beach destination known for its lighthouse and relaxing atmosphere.Kovalam Beach, located 15-18 km from Thiruvananthapuram in Kerala, is a world-renowned coastal town featuring three, adjacent crescent-shaped beaches (Lighthouse, Hawa, and Samudra). Known for shallow, calm waters, it is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and Ayurvedic massages. The best time to visit is from October to March, offering pleasant weather for beach activities.

Location: Kovalam, Kerala
