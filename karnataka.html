Karnataka Tourist Places

1. Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Karnataka. It is known for its beautiful architecture and royal history.Mysore Palace (Amba Vilas Palace) in Karnataka is India's second most visited tourist attraction after the Taj Mahal, famous for its magnificent Indo-Saracenic architecture, opulent interiors, and the dazzling illumination of nearly 100,000 bulbs, especially during the annual Dasara festival. Built between 1897 and 1912, this former Wodeyar royal residence features intricate carvings, stained glass, and a stunning, jewel-encrusted golden throne

Location: Mysuru, Karnataka

2. Hampi

Hampi is a historic city famous for its ancient temples and ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire.The Lotus Mahal (or Kamal Mahal/Chitrangi Mahal) is a prominent, two-storied secular structure in Hampi’s Zenana Enclosure, used by royal women of the Vijayanagara Empire. Known for its lotus-like, arched architecture, this 14th-century building blends Hindu and Islamic styles, featuring open pavilions, balconies, and intricate plasterwork

Location: Vijayanagara District, Karnataka

View Location

3. Coorg

Coorg is a beautiful hill station known for coffee plantations and scenic nature.Coorg (Kodagu), often called the "Scotland of India" and the "Kashmir of the South," is a breathtaking hill station in Karnataka's Western Ghats renowned for its lush coffee plantations, misty mountains, and rich biodiversity. Known for its cool climate, Kodava culture, and spice plantations, key attractions include Abbey Falls, Raja’s Seat, and the Dubare Elephant Camp.

Location: Kodagu District, Karnataka

View Location

4. Jog Falls

Jog Falls is one of the highest waterfalls in India and a popular tourist attraction.Jog Falls, located in Karnataka's Shimoga district, is India's second-highest plunge waterfall, dropping 253 meters (830 feet) in four distinct cascades (Raja, Rani, Roarer, Rocket). Best visited during the monsoon (July-September), this spectacular, fast-flowing waterfall is a major attraction for nature lovers.

Location: Shivamogga District, Karnataka
